When Vikram lander of Chandrayaan-3 touched down on the Moon's surface on August 23, it kicked up a bunch of lunar dust and rocks, creating a bright area around the lander, as it was expected.

How AI was used in Chandraayan-3

The precision landing was made possible by recalibrating the descending trajectory and recording the lunar surface with the help of AI-powered instruments like velocimeters, altimeters, and accelerometers. The three primary parts of Chandrayaan-3 were the propulsion module, the lander (Vikram), and the rover (Pragyan).


Technologies Used in creation of Chandraayan-3

Chandrayaan-3 is a highly complex mission that uses a variety of cutting-edge technologies. Some of the key technologies used in the mission include:

Propulsion systems Chandrayaan-3 is powered by a combination of solid-fuel and liquid-fuel rockets. The solid-fuel rockets are used for the initial launch and ascent to orbit, while the liquid-fuel rockets are used for orbital maneuvers and the soft landing on the Moon.

Onboard sensors Chandrayaan-3 is equipped with a variety of onboard sensors, including cameras, spectrometers, and magnetometers. These sensors are used to collect data about the Moon's surface, environment, and gravity field.

Communication technology Chandrayaan-3 communicates with Earth using a variety of communication systems, including S-band, X-band, and UHF. These systems allow the spacecraft to transmit data to Earth and receive commands from mission control.

Navigation, guidance, and control (NGC) system Chandrayaan-3's NGC system is responsible for the spacecraft's navigation, guidance, and control. The NGC system uses a combination of sensors, computers, and actuators to ensure that the spacecraft remains on its desired trajectory.

AI-powered landing system Chandrayaan-3's lander uses an AI-powered landing system to achieve a soft landing on the Moon. The AI system uses data from the lander's sensors to identify and avoid obstacles on the Moon's surface.



New navigation system The new navigation system used in Chandrayaan-3 is called the Terrain Relative Navigation System (TRNS). TRNS is an AI-powered system that uses a variety of sensors, including cameras, altimeters, and accelerometers, to generate a real-time 3D map of the Moon's surface. The system then uses this map to guide the spacecraft to a safe landing spot. TRNS is a significant improvement over previous navigation systems used for lunar landings. Previous systems were based on pre-programmed maps of the Moon's surface, which could be inaccurate or outdated. TRNS, on the other hand, generates a real-time map of the Moon's surface, which allows it to compensate for any changes in the landscape.

New rover The new rover used in Chandrayaan-3 is called the Pragyan rover. Pragyan is a six-wheeled rover that is equipped with a variety of sensors and instruments, including cameras, spectrometers, and a ground penetrating radar. The rover is designed to explore the Moon's surface and collect data about its geology, mineralogy, and environment.

New landerThe new lander used in Chandrayaan-3 is called the Vikram lander. Vikram is a four-legged lander that is equipped with a variety of sensors and instruments, including cameras, altimeters, and accelerometers. The lander is designed to deliver the Pragyan rover to the Moon's surface safely.

The new technologies used in Chandrayaan-3 represent a significant advancement in India's space capabilities. These technologies will allow India to conduct more sophisticated lunar missions in the future.
